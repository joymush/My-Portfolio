How to cook classic kenyan Beef stew

ingredients

  • 1/2kg beef,cut into bite sizes
  • 1 cup of water
  • 4 tablespoons vegetable or olive oil
  • 1 big red onion or 2 small ones,diced
  • 5 garlic cloves,minced
  • 1 inch first ginger,minced
  • 3-4 fresh tomatoes,diced
  • 1 teaspoon tomatopaste
  • 2 carrots,diced
  • 5 small potatoes,diced
  • 3 bay leave
  • 1/2 teaspoon paparika powder

Instructions

  1. In a saucepan,place meat,ginger,garlic and 1cup of water.Boil the meat for roughly 3o minutes on medium heat
  2. Separate meat from broth and setaside(you'll need the broth later for the stew)
  3. In a separate saucepan,heat vegetable oil on medium heat,fry onions till soft.Add beef and fry till it starts to brown.Add tomatoes and tomato paste and cook till the fresh tomatoes have softened.Add the spices;curry powder and paparika and mix well.Let the mixture simmer for a minute or two for the spices to mix well with the beef.
  4. Add carrots and potatoes and stir well,again let the mixture simmer about five minutes stirring each minute to avoid sticking in the pan.Once everything has mixed in well add the broth you had set aside earlier.Stir well and throw in the bay leaves,lower the heat to low and simmer about 45 minutes or until the meat is tender stirrimg from time to time.Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Garnish with fresh corinder and serve hot with rice,ugali or chapati.
